Slow. Down.

I had to tell myself this while presenting at #KEYS2026 conference this week. I’ve realized it applies just as much to my daily work as a software developer, too.

**Friday Foundations (Week 3)**

🧠 One idea that stuck with me:

In the SaaS/B2B space, understanding where customers are coming from is essential. We must ensure the right features get prioritized and that we carry the right context throughout the SDLC.

🔄 It challenged a previous assumption I had:

It’s easy to stay in "dev mode", where I’m focused on clean code, meeting requirements, and following design rules.

👀 What I’m starting to see instead:

Even after the design and estimation are complete, it’s worth questioning whether the solution truly serves end users: "Does this align with how guidance instructs our customers?", "Is there a simpler way to deliver this behavior?", "Will these changes be easy to follow, internally and externally?"

⚙️ Why this matters:

I tend to want to complete my assigned work and move on, without pausing to consider alternatives or industry-specific requirements that could affect what we ultimately deliver to customers.

👨‍💻 How I’m applying it:

Moving fast doesn’t mean moving well. Quality is usually the first thing to suffer when speed takes priority. Whether I’m presenting to a room or writing code, the instinct to rush is the same, and so is the fix.
